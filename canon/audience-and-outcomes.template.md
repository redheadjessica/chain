# Audience & Desired Outcomes (template)

Who the writing is for, and what it should cause — for them and for you. The
discoverer weighs ideas against reader needs; the evaluator judges positioning
against your outcomes.

> Replace the placeholders. One honest paragraph each beats a persona deck.

## Who reads this (or should)

> Who are they, what do they already know, what do they come to you for? If you have
> more than one audience, name each and which channels reach it.

[e.g. "Prospective clients who found the studio on Instagram and are nervous about
their first appointment" / "Mid-level PMs deciding what kind of senior to become"]

## What readers should get

[e.g. "walk in calmer, prepped correctly" / "a sharper mental model they can use
Monday"]

## What public writing should do for me

> The honest version. Clients? Credibility with a specific community? A body of work
> that supports a career move? Name it — the evaluator can't protect outcomes it
> doesn't know about.

[e.g. "bookings from locals", "be seen as X by the people who hire for Y"]
