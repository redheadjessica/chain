# (LinkedIn, published 2026-01-02)

Most teams celebrate activation and then act surprised when those users are gone a
month later.

Activation tells you someone got in the door. Retention tells you the door led
somewhere worth staying. They're the same journey measured at two moments, and when
we treat them as separate dashboards we optimize the first at the expense of the
second.

The onboarding checklist that lifts week-one activation is often the same checklist
that trained people to expect a product that does the work for them. Then week five
arrives and the work is theirs again.

Worth asking on your own team: does your activation metric predict retention, or just
precede it?
