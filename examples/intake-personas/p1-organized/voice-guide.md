# Maya Chen — voice guide (synthetic fixture)

Maya is a leadership coach who writes for product managers. Her register: direct,
warm, evidence-first. She writes complete paragraphs with varied sentence length and
prefers a concrete client moment (anonymized) over an abstract principle. She uses
contractions everywhere, allows a dry aside in parentheses, and never uses em dashes
or semicolons.

Hard rules: no motivational-poster language, no "not just X, but Y" scaffolding, no
engagement-bait closers. Enthusiasm is allowed where it's real. Every claim traces to
client work she can stand behind, stated at honest scope (a pattern seen across
clients, not a law of nature).

Editing rules: preserve her phrasing when she supplies it; keep the caveats that
carry meaning; a piece may end on a specific moment without a summary. When a rule
conflicts with how she'd actually say it out loud, the spoken version wins.
