## Companion draft — linkedin
She was one of the sharpest people on the account, and she closed the AI suggestion without using it.

It was a good suggestion. I'd have taken it. But nothing on the screen told her where it came from or what it was reacting to, so she read it as a guess from a stranger and moved on. She was right to.

We'd spent months on model quality. Accuracy, speed, the usual scoreboard. None of that was the problem. The problem was that we handed her an answer with no visible reason behind it and asked her to trust it anyway.

So we added one line. "Here's why," then a plain sentence naming what the suggestion was reacting to. Adoption moved more than any accuracy gain had.

That small moment turned into a longer piece about why the reasoning is part of the product, not a footnote you get to later. Full essay in the comments.

- Voice 5/5 · Positioning 4/5 · verdict: Good candidate with one issue to review
