# (LinkedIn, published 2026-01-09)

The hardest part of shipping an AI feature isn't the model. It's making what the
model did legible to someone who didn't build it.

I watched a smart, non-technical user stare at a perfectly good AI suggestion and not
trust it, because nothing told them where it came from or what it was reacting to. We
added one line of "here's why" and adoption moved more than any accuracy gain had.

Legibility is a product surface, not a footnote. If people can't see the reasoning,
they treat the output as a coin flip, and they're right to.
